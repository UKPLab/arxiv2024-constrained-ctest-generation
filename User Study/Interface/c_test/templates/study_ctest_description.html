What are C-tests?

The C in C-test stands for its origin in the cloze test. Although the cloze test (a common gap exercise) is widely used, the setup contains several weaknesses such as the small number of gaps and the ambiguity of the solution. The C-test was developed as an alternative which addresses most of the weaknesses of the cloze test. In the following, you can see an example C-test (Note: this is only a mock-up for demonstration purposes).

Call me Ishmael. Some years ago—never mind how long precisely—having little or no money in my purse, and nothing particular to interest me on shore, I thought I would sail about a little and see the watery part of the world. It i a wa I ha of driv off t spleen a regulating t circulation. Whenever I fi myself gro grim ab the mou; whenever i is a da, drizzly Nove in m soul; when I fi myself involu pausing bef coffin wareh, and bringing up the rear of every funeral I meet; and especially whenever my hypos get such an upper hand of me, that it requires a strong moral principle to prevent me from deliberately stepping into the street, and methodically knocking people’s hats off—then, I account it high time to get to sea as soon as I can.

Usually, the generation strategy of C-Tests is fix. However, this results in a low variablility of C-Tests. In this study, our goal is to evaluate various new strategies for C-Test generation. During participation, you will be tasked with solving various C-Tests of different difficulty. Do not worry if you are not able to solve many gaps, it will still result in helpful feedback for our study. After each C-Test we will further ask you to assess your performance:
